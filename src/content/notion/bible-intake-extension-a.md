## 2. 文字介紹

「聖經手」圖解是一個容易記憶的工具，突顯從聖經學習的五種方法。鼓勵你使用它來幫助自己的靈性成長，並啟發他人。The Word Hand illustration is an easy-to-remember tool highlighting five methods of learning from the Bible. We encourage you to use it to spur your own spiritual growth and to inspire others.

**聆聽 (Hear)** 牧師和教師從神的話語教導，為我們對聖經帶來新的洞見。最弱的手指(尾指)代表聆聽,因為我們只能保留所聽到的百分之五。HEARING pastors and teachers teach from the Word provides fresh insight into the Scriptures. The weakest finger (the pinkie) represents hearing, because we retain only five percent of what we hear. (Rom 羅馬書 10:17)

**閱讀 (Read)** 讓我們對聖經有整體的認識,也是每日靈修的基礎。這由無名指代表。我們一般能保留所閱讀的百分之十五。READING gives us an overview of the Bible and is the foundation of a daily quiet time. This is represented by the ring finger. We generally retain 15 percent of what we read. (Rev 啟示錄 1:3)

**查考 (Study)** 聖經加深我們的信念。這需要更多的時間和努力，但能深入認識聖經的真你。大多數人能保留所研讀的百分之三十五。這由中指代表。STUDYING the Scriptures deepens our convictions. It requires greater time and effort but results in increased knowledge of the Bible. Most people retain 35 percent of what they study. This is represented by the middle finger.  (Act 使徒行傳17:11)

**背誦 (Memorize)** 神的話語使我們能夠運用聖經，即「聖靈的寶劍」(以弗所書6:17)，勝過試探，並隨時有經文可用來服事他人。食指是我們最強壯的手指，代表背誦。如果我們持續溫習，我們能記住百分之百所背誦的內容。背誦聖經前最好先詳細查考，以清楚認識其中的教導。MEMORIZING God’s Word enables us to use Scripture, “the Sword of the Spirit” (Ephesians 6:17), to overcome temptations and to have verses readily available for ministering to others. The index finger, our strongest finger, represents memorization. We remember 100 percent of what we memorize if we consistently review it. (Psalm 詩篇119:9-11)

**默想 (Meditate)** 是伴隨其他四種吸收聖經方法的過程。這就是為何默想被分配給拇指。只有拇指能夠觸及其他四隻手指。透過在聆聽、閱讀、研讀和背誦時默想神的話語,我們發現它在我們裡面的轉化大能。MEDITATION is the process that accompanies each of the other four methods of Scripture intake. This is why meditation is assigned to the thumb. Only the thumb can touch all the other four fingers. By meditating on God’s Word as we hear, read, study, and memorize, we discover its transforming power at work in us.  (Psalm 詩篇1:2-3)
